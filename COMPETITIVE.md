# Plato — Competitive wedge

## Landscape

| Category | Examples | Strength | Weakness vs Plato |
|----------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Paper menus | Print shops | Familiar | One language, no photos, reprint cost |
| PDF QR | Free QR generators | Cheap | Ugly, static, not multilingual UX |
| QR menu builders | MenuTiger, FineDine, mlqrmenu, TableQR | Multi-lang exists | Often generic, weak photo story, not “fun” |
| POS suites | Toast, Square | Ordering + payments | Heavy, expensive, menu UX secondary |
| Review platforms | Yelp, Google | Photos + social proof | Not ordered by dish; ads; not the official menu |

**Market truth:** Multilingual digital menus already exist and prove demand (higher engagement, higher order value with photos). Plato does not invent the category — it **wins the experience**.

---

## Positioning statement

> For food trucks and casual restaurants that serve bilingual or tourist guests,  
> **Plato** is the **living menu**  
> that makes every dish understandable in the guest’s language with real plate photos.  
> Unlike PDF QR codes or bloated POS menus,  
> Plato is **phone-beautiful, language-first, and dish-photo true**.

## Why guests prefer Plato to Yelp for “what does it look like?”

1. **Scoped to this restaurant’s menu right now**  
2. **One row = one dish** with price and description  
3. **No ads, no wrong restaurant, no parking lot photos**  
4. **Language matches the description of ingredients**

Yelp remains for reviews. Plato owns **decision at the point of order**.

---

## Why owners prefer Plato to “just Toast”

- Many trucks don’t want full POS swap  
- Plato sits **beside** cash / Square  
- Faster story: inclusion + photos + modern feel  
- Lower price for menu-only job  

Later: integrate ordering when they ask.

## Moat (honest)

| Soft moat | How |
|-----------|-----|
| Content | Their menu + photo library lives on Plato |
| Habit | Guests learn “scan for Español” at favorite trucks |
| Workflow | Daily sold-out + photo approve |
| Brand in niche | “The Spanish-friendly menu” in a city corridor |

Hard tech moat: none early. **Speed, taste, and distribution** win.

## Wedge market (beachhead)

1. **Spanish/English food trucks** in 1–2 metro areas  
2. Expand to other bilingual pairs (EN/ZH, EN/KO) in same product shell  
3. Then multi-unit casual chains  

Do not launch as “global menu OS.” Launch as **“your truck, finally clear in Spanish.”**
