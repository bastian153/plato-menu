# Plato — Sales script & go-to-market

## Who to sell first

**Ideal first customers**

- Food trucks / night market stalls  
- Casual taquerías, pupuserías, Chinese takeout, etc. with **mixed English/Spanish** traffic  
- Tourist-area spots with English-only boards  
- Owners who already use a **QR → PDF** and know it’s weak  

**Avoid first:** Fine dining with printed books only and no phone culture; enterprise chains (long sales).

---

## 2-minute in-person pitch (food truck)

**You:** “Quick question — do people ever struggle with the English menu?”

*(They almost always say yes, or shrug “sometimes.”)*

**You:** “I built a living menu. Guest scans, taps Español, sees real photos of each plate. Not a PDF. Can I show you on your phone?”

*(Open demo → switch EN/ES → open Al Pastor → swipe photos.)*

**You:** “You update sold-out in one tap. Prices change without reprinting. Thirty-nine a month for trucks. If you want, I’ll load your real dishes this week for free.”

**Close:** “Want me to set up El Sol with your 8 best sellers by Friday?”

---

## Objection handling

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| “We already have a QR” | “Most QRs are PDFs. Do guests get Spanish descriptions and photos per dish?” |
| “My staff can translate” | “At a busy rush they can’t. The phone can.” |
| “Too expensive” | “One wrong order or lost taco sale a day covers the month. Reprinting paper costs more than you think.” |
| “I don’t have photos” | “Text me 10 plates this weekend — or use guest photos after you open.” |
| “Old people won’t scan” | “Paper can stay. This is for the people already on their phone — often your Spanish and tourist guests.” |
| “I’ll think about it” | “I’ll send the demo link. If 3 staff open it in Spanish this week, you’ll see why it matters.” |

---

## Demo path (always the same)

1. Landing (optional) — 5 seconds  
2. **Guest menu** — switch to **ES**  
3. Open **Al Pastor** — swipe guest photos  
4. **Help me choose** — fun moment  
5. **Owner** — mark sold out → back to menu → badge shows  
6. Price: **$39 truck / $99 restaurant**

## Channels

| Channel | Tactic |
|---------|--------|
| In person | Night markets, truck pods, commissary kitchens |
| Facebook | “[City] food truck owners”, restaurant operator groups |
| Hispanic chambers | Short demo at mixers |
| Partners | Square freelancers, local web designers, POS installers |
| Content | Before/after: English PDF vs Plato Español (screenshot ads later) |

**Do not:** Run consumer “download our app” ads. Guests never install. Sell owners.

---

## Onboarding checklist (first restaurant)

1. Collect dish list + prices (photo of paper menu is fine)  
2. EN + ES descriptions (AI draft → owner edit)  
3. 1 photo per dish minimum  
4. Print QR sticker for window / register  
5. Train: “sold out” button + language stats  
6. 7-day check-in: any confusion?  

---

## Pilot offer (first 10 customers)

- **$0 for 30 days** then $39  
- Or **$99 setup** includes menu build + stickers, first month free  
- Case study rights: photo of truck + quote

## What “good” sales week looks like

- 15 demos in person  
- 5 links sent  
- 2 pilots live  
- 1 paid conversion  

Volume + demos beat perfect ads early.
